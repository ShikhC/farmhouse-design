Plumbing Notes

Water flows by gravity from rooftop tank to all fixtures. Underground drain pipes must be laid BEFORE the floor slab is poured. The vent pipe (2" going above roof) is critical — without it, toilet drains slowly due to air lock. Floor drain channel runs the length of the storage for wash-down cleaning. 4 roof downpipes handle monsoon rainwater — directed away from the building to prevent plinth erosion.

Why Install Now?

Conduits/sleeves cost ₹3-5K during construction.

Doing it LATER means:
• Breaking walls (₹15-20K damage)
• Core-cutting slab (₹10K + weakens slab)
• Re-plastering + painting
Save ₹40-50K by planning ahead

Future Provisions — Install During Construction

These provisions cost almost nothing to install now (just PVC pipes + wire embedded during construction) but save ₹40,000-50,000 later. The draw wire inside each conduit allows future cables to be pulled through without opening walls. Pipe sleeves in the slab allow future plumbing without core-cutting. Solar conduit connects roof panels to ground floor inverter. All are capped/sealed to prevent concrete entry and debris — opened only when needed.

Key Principle: MEP Work is FREE If Done at the Right Time

Embedding a conduit during wall construction costs ₹0 extra (the mason just lays it as he builds). Doing the same work AFTER the wall is plastered costs ₹500-1000 per meter (chasing + re-plastering + re-painting). This tab shows EXACTLY when each MEP task must happen.
