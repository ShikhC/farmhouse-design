# Contractor Brief — Farm Storage Construction

**Kaam:** Farmhouse Storage Building
**Jagah:** Mithanpur Urf Naimtulla Nagar, Moradabad, UP
**Size:** 20 ft × 25 ft, 12 ft unchai, 3 ft plinth

---

## BUILDING AT A GLANCE

```
    Total Width:   20 feet (chaudai)
    Total Depth:   25 feet (lambai)  
    Plinth Height:  3 feet (chabootra - flooding ke liye)
    Room Height:   12 feet (floor to slab)
    Front Shade:    5 feet aage nikla hua (cantilever)
    
    Walls:  Bahar = 9 inch (do eet)
            Andar = 4.5 inch (ek eet)
```

## STEP-BY-STEP CONSTRUCTION ORDER

### PHASE 1: Foundation (Neev) — Week 1-2

1. **Layout marking** (Khaaka lagana)
   - Mark column positions with lime powder
   - 8 columns ONLY — ALL in walls:
     - Back wall: 2 (left corner + right corner only)
     - Side walls: 2 (one on each side wall, at 9ft from front)
     - Front wall: 4 (left corner, 7.5ft mark, 17.5ft mark, right corner)
   - NO columns in the middle of the storage floor
   - NO shade columns at front (shade is pure cantilever)
   - Middle beam position: 9ft from front wall (this aligns with the first floor room partition above)

2. **Dig pits** (Gadda khodna)
   - Each pit: 4ft × 4ft × 4ft deep
   - 8 pits total (all at wall positions)
   - Keep sides straight, bottom level

3. **PCC bed** (Neev ka bed)
   - Pour 6" M15 concrete at pit bottom
   - Level it with patti
   - Leave for 2 days

4. **RCC Footings** (Neev ki slab)
   - Size: 3ft × 3ft × 12" thick
   - Steel: 12mm sariya @ 6" dono taraf
   - M20 concrete (1 cement : 1.5 sand : 3 gitti)
   - Cure 7 days (paani dalna)

5. **Column stubs** (Pillar ki shuruat)
   - Raise column bars from footing to +3ft (plinth level)
   - 4 nos. 12mm bars per column + 8mm ring @ 6" c/c
   - Concrete up to plinth level

---

### PHASE 2: Plinth (Chabootra) — Week 3-4

6. **Plinth walls** (Chabootre ki deewaar)
   - 9" brick wall connecting all columns
   - Build all 4 sides + internal cross walls under beam lines
   - Height: 3ft from natural ground
   - Use 1:6 mortar (cement:sand)

7. **Earth filling** (Mitti bharna)
   - Fill inside with murum/mitti
   - **IMPORTANT:** Fill in 6" layers only
   - Water each layer, then compact with thamper
   - Do NOT fill all at once — layers are critical
   - Total: ~2.5 ft fill height

8. **PCC over fill** (Farsh ka base)
   - 3-4 inch M15 concrete over compacted earth
   - Level properly with wooden patti
   - Cure 3-4 days

9. **Floor slab** (Farsh ki RCC slab)
   - Lay welded wire mesh (jali): 6mm @ 6" × 6"
   - Keep mesh on 1" cover blocks (support)
   - Pour 4" M20 concrete
   - Finish smooth with trowel
   - Cure minimum 14 days

---

### PHASE 3: Walls (Deewaarein) — Week 5-7

10. **Raise columns** (Pillar banana)
    - Cast remaining column height (12 ft, from plinth to slab)
    - Use steel formwork (shuttering)
    - Vibrate concrete properly — no honeycomb!
    - Cure 7 days (wrap with wet cloth)

11. **External walls** (Bahari deewaar)
    - 9 inch thick (do eet)
    - Leave openings:
      - Front: 10ft × 10ft (shutter) + 3ft × 7ft (gate)
      - Sides: 4 nos. vents (9" × 12") at 10ft height
      - Back: 2 nos. vents (9" × 12") at 11ft height
    - Mortar: 1:6 (cement:sand)

12. **Future window provisions** (Khidki ki jagah chhodni)
    - **DO NOT leave actual openings** (security)
    - Cast RCC lintel at these positions:
      - Left wall: 2 positions (at 8ft and 18ft from front, 3ft height from floor)
      - Right wall: 2 positions (same)
      - Back wall: 1 position (center)
    - Lintel size: 9" × 9", extending 6" beyond opening width
    - Opening size (fill with brick): 4ft wide × 3ft high
    - Fill with HALF BRICK only (not bonded tight) — easy to remove later

13. **Internal walls** (Andarki deewaar)
    - 4.5 inch thick (ek eet)
    - Stair partition: from front wall going 10ft inside (left side)
    - Toilet enclosure: 3ft × 5ft under staircase
    - Use 1:4 mortar (stronger for thin walls)

---

### PHASE 4: Staircase (Seedhi) — Week 7-8

14. **Dog-legged staircase** (Seedhi — do dhaara)

    ```
    IMPORTANT DIMENSIONS:
    
    Riser (unchai):     7 inch (175mm)
    Tread (chaudai):   10 inch (250mm)  
    Width per flight:   3 ft 6 inch (3.5 ft)
    Total rise:        12 ft
    Risers:            20 (10 per flight)
    Treads:             9 per flight
    ```

    - Type: Waist slab (patli slab ke upar seedhi)
    - Waist slab: 5" thick RCC
    - Steel: 10mm main @ 6" + 8mm distribution @ 8"
    - Landing: 7.5ft × 3ft at middle (half height)
    - **KEY:** Pour waist slab + steps in one go (ek baar mein dhalna)
    - Cure 14 days

15. **Handrails** (Railing)
    - Both sides of staircase
    - 1.5" MS pipe
    - Height: 3 ft from step nosing
    - Embed in concrete (pocket in steps)

---

### PHASE 5: Roof Slab (Chhat) — Week 9-10

16. **Beam formwork** (Beam ka shuttering)
    - **TWO deep beams: 9" × 20"** — both span full 20ft width:
      1. Back beam: along back wall (between 2 corner columns)
      2. Middle beam: at 9ft from front wall (between 2 side wall columns)
    - Standard beams: 9" × 15" on side walls and front wall
    - Middle beam is at 9ft from front — this is where the first floor room wall will sit
    - Support with strong props (6ft × 4ft patti)
    - Check levels with water level / spirit level

17. **Slab formwork** (Chhat ka shuttering)
    - Cover entire 20ft × 25ft area
    - PLUS 5ft cantilever at front (shade projection)
    - Leave staircase opening: 7.5ft × 3ft at front-left
    - Shuttering must be LEVEL (check multiple points)
    - Support every 3-4 ft with vertical props

18. **Steel laying** (Sariya daalna)
    - Standard beams (side walls + front): 3-16mm (bottom) + 2-12mm (top) + 8mm stirrups @ 6"
    - **BOTH deep beams (back + middle, 9"×20"):** 4-16mm (bottom) + 2-16mm (top) + 8mm stirrups @ 5" near supports
    - Slab: 10mm @ 6" (main) + 8mm @ 8" (distribution) — NOTE: slab is **6" thick**
    - Cantilever: Extra top bars (10mm @ 5") anchored **10ft** into main slab
    - Cantilever tapers: 6" thick at wall, 4" at tip
    - **NO COLUMNS at front edge** — pure cantilever
    - **CRITICAL:** Cantilever bars on TOP, not bottom!

19. **Concrete pour** (Dhulai)
    - M20 concrete (1:1.5:3)
    - Pour beams and slab together in ONE go (cold joint nahi hona chahiye)
    - Start from one corner, move systematically
    - Vibrate well (needle vibrator use karein)
    - Finish top surface level and smooth
    - Start time: early morning (avoid afternoon heat)

20. **Curing** (Paani dena)
    - Keep slab wet for minimum 21 days
    - Make ponding walls with mud (2" border)
    - Fill water on top — do not let it dry
    - **DO NOT remove shuttering before 21 days** (28 days is best)

---

### PHASE 6: External Works — Week 10-12

21. **Tractor ramp** (Tractor ka dhalaan)
    - Width: 12 ft
    - Length: 8 ft only (steep OK — sirf tractor aayega, trolley bahar shutter pe rahegi)
    - Slope: ~1:2.7 (3ft rise in 8ft)
    - Base: Compacted earth + 6" RCC slab
    - Surface: Grooved/textured (anti-slip for tires)
    - Side walls: 1.5ft high, 4.5" brick
    - **IMPORTANT:** Ramp ke upar aur neeche ka mod gol hona chahiye (rounded transition)
      — warna tractor ka pet ragdega

22. **Gate steps** (Seedhiyan - gate ke liye)
    - 5 steps: 7" riser × 10" tread × 3ft wide
    - Small landing (3ft × 3ft) at top
    - Material: Brick + cement plaster or RCC

23. **Soak pit** (Soakpit / gaddha)
    - Location: 10ft from building (back or side)
    - Dig: 4ft diameter × 6ft deep
    - Wall: Brick with gaps (honeycomb — paani rishne ke liye)
    - Bottom: 1ft gravel/stone filling (no concrete)
    - Top: RCC cover slab + inspection lid
    - Connect: 4" PVC pipe from toilet (1:40 slope minimum)

24. **Water tank installation** (Paani ki tanki)
    - 500L HDPE tank on roof (above toilet area)
    - Platform: MS angle frame or concrete pedestal
    - Connect submersible pump → 1" GI pipe → tank
    - Overflow pipe → drain away from building

---

### PHASE 7: Finishing — Week 11-12

25. **Plumbing** (Nalkool ka kaam)
    - **BEFORE plastering:** Embed pipes in walls
    - Supply: 1/2" CPVC from tank → toilet
    - Drain: 4" PVC from commode → soak pit
    - Floor drain in storage → front (under ramp)
    - External tap on side wall

26. **Plastering** (Plaster)
    - External: 1:6 cement mortar, 12-15mm thick
    - Internal: 1:4 cement mortar, 12mm thick
    - Ceiling: 1:4, 10mm thick
    - Cure 7 days

27. **Flooring** (Farsh ki finishing)
    - Storage: IPS (cement + marble chips, machine polish)
    - OR: Plain cement with hardener (red/grey)
    - Toilet: Anti-skid tiles (2ft × 2ft tiles)
    - Ramp: Leave grooved (no finish — better grip)

28. **Installations** (Lagana)
    - Rolling shutter: 10ft × 10ft, MS galvanized
    - Gate: 3ft × 7ft, MS with lock
    - Toilet door: 2ft × 6.5ft
    - Vent grills: 6 nos., MS with wire mesh
    - Handrails: MS pipe staircase
    - Commode + basin + taps

29. **Painting / Waterproofing**
    - Roof: China mosaic (broken tiles + white cement) for waterproofing
    - External: White weatherproof paint (2 coats)
    - Internal: White distemper (2 coats)
    - Toilet: Waterproof cement paint

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | What To Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Earth fill in one layer | Settles later, cracks floor | Fill in 6" layers, compact each |
| Removing shuttering early | Slab can sag or crack | Wait minimum 21 days (28 best) |
| Cantilever bars at bottom | Slab tip will droop/break | Cantilever reinforcement goes on TOP |
| Concrete in afternoon heat | Sets too fast, weak joints | Pour early morning or evening |
| No vibration | Honeycomb in concrete (weak spots) | Use needle vibrator for all RCC |
| Joints in slab pour | Cold joints = weak slab | Pour full slab in one session |
| Thin cover on steel | Steel rusts → concrete spalls | Maintain 1" min cover everywhere |
| No curing | Concrete reaches only 50% strength | Wet cure 21+ days, keep moist |
| Plinth not compacted | Floor sinks later | Compact in layers, water each |
| Wrong slope on drain | Toilet doesn't flush properly | Min 1:40 slope (2.5% fall) |
| Staircase too steep | Unsafe for senior workers | Keep riser at 7", tread at 10" |
| Adding columns inside storage | Blocks tractor movement | Middle beam (9"×20") spans full 20ft — NO internal columns needed |

## QUALITY CHECKS

**Before pouring any concrete, verify:**
1. ☐ Steel is correct size and spacing
2. ☐ Cover blocks are in place (1" cover)
3. ☐ Formwork is level and properly supported
4. ☐ No loose bars or ties
5. ☐ Water arrangement ready for curing next day

**Before raising walls, verify:**
1. ☐ Plinth is level (check with water tube level)
2. ☐ Column positions are correct
3. ☐ Opening sizes marked on floor with chalk

**Before roof slab pour:**
1. ☐ All beams and slab steel verified by structural person
2. ☐ Staircase opening boxed properly (7.5ft × 3ft)
3. ☐ Cantilever bars on TOP (not bottom!)
4. ☐ Electrical conduits if any (future provision)
5. ☐ Sufficient workers ready (8-10 minimum for 500 sft slab)
6. ☐ Ready-mix or mixer + materials for non-stop pour
