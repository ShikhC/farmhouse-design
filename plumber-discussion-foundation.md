# Plumber Discussion — Foundation & Floor Phase

**Date:** 11 June 2026
**Context:** Foundation is being laid. This is the ONLY chance to get underground plumbing right.
**Purpose:** Clear all expectations with plumber before floor slab is poured.

---

## ⚠️ WHY THIS CONVERSATION IS CRITICAL

Once the floor slab (4" RCC with wire mesh) is poured, **you CANNOT access underground pipes without breaking the floor** (₹15,000-20,000 damage + 2 weeks delay). Everything below must be 100% correct NOW.

---

## PART 1: WHAT THE PLUMBER MUST DO DURING FOUNDATION PHASE

### 1.1 Underground Drain Pipe (Toilet → Soak Pit)

**The most critical piece — discuss these EXACT specifications:**

| Item | Specification | Why it matters |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| Pipe | 4" PVC SWR (Soil, Waste, Rain grade) | Regular PVC will crack under earth load |
| Length | ~15-18 feet (from toilet position to soak pit) | Measure and confirm on site |
| Depth | 1.5 feet below floor slab level | Deep enough to not interfere with floor mesh |
| Bedding | 6" sand bed below pipe | Prevents point loading, pipe won't crack |
| Slope | **1:40 minimum (2.5% fall)** | Water MUST flow by gravity — this is NON-NEGOTIABLE |
| Joints | Solvent cement (PVC weld) — NOT rubber rings for underground | Rubber rings can leak underground |
| Direction | From toilet (front-left corner) to soak pit (10ft from building, back or side) | Shortest path, no sharp turns |
| Bends | Maximum 2 bends, use 45° bends (NOT 90°) | 90° bends cause blockages |
| End at toilet | Stub up 6" above floor level (for future commode connection) | Will connect to commode P-trap later |
| End at soak pit | Enter soak pit wall at top (below cover level) | Effluent enters high, seeps down |

**Questions to ask the plumber:**
- "Pipe ka slope kaise check karoge?" (How will you verify the slope?)
  - Answer should be: spirit level + straight edge on pipe, OR water test
- "Sand bed kitna thick?" — Must be 6" minimum
- "Joints mein kya lagaoge?" — Must be solvent cement, NOT just rubber gasket
- "Pipe ke upar kya daaloge before mitti?" — Should cover with sand first, then earth

### 1.2 Soak Pit Construction

| Item | Specification |
|------|--------------|
| Location | Minimum 10 feet from building (any side, preferably downhill) |
| Distance from water source | Minimum 50 feet from borewell/submersible |
| Diameter | 4 feet |
| Depth | 6 feet |
| Wall type | Brick HONEYCOMB (gaps between bricks for water to seep into earth) |
| Bottom | NO concrete — open earth with 1 foot gravel/stone aggregate |
| Cover | RCC slab (4.5ft × 4.5ft × 3") with CI inspection opening (12"×12") |
| Inlet | 4" PVC pipe enters near the top of the pit (1ft below cover) |

**Questions to ask:**
- "Honeycomb brickwork samajh mein aaya? Gap chodna hai!" — Plumber must leave gaps, NOT solid wall
- "Bottom mein concrete nahi dalna — sirf badi gitti" — No concrete at bottom
- "Inspection cover lagayega?" — Must have access for future cleaning

### 1.3 Floor Drain Channel

| Item | Specification |

## PART 2: THINGS TO VERIFY BEFORE FLOOR SLAB IS POURED

### Checklist (do this with plumber 1 day before dhulai/pour):

- [ ] **Slope test:** Pour a bucket of water at the toilet drain stub — does it flow all the way to the soak pit? If not, slope is wrong. FIX BEFORE POUR.
- [ ] **Pipe not crushed:** Walk the entire drain line — no point should be compressed by earth/stones sitting on it.
- [ ] **Sand bed continuous:** 6" sand bed under entire pipe length (not just at ends).
- [ ] **Joints sealed:** Pour water at high end — check EVERY joint for leaks over 30 minutes.
- [ ] **Floor trap in place:** CI trap set at correct level (flush with future floor).
- [ ] **Drain channel formwork ready:** Wooden/steel formwork for the 6"×3" channel placed before pour.
- [ ] **Plinth exit pipes in place:** 4" PVC pipes through plinth wall at all 4 corners + front drain exit.
- [ ] **Soak pit complete and connected:** Pipe enters pit wall, pit is covered.
- [ ] **Stub-up at toilet:** 4" pipe sticking 6" above PCC level where commode will go.
- [ ] **No debris in pipes:** Run water through to confirm pipes are clear.
- [ ] **Photos taken:** Photograph entire underground layout BEFORE covering with concrete. You'll never see it again.

## PART 3: COMMON PLUMBER MISTAKES TO WATCH FOR

| Mistake | Why it's bad | What to insist on |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| No slope or reverse slope | Sewage won't flow, toilet won't flush properly | Measure with spirit level — minimum 1:40 |
| Using thin/regular PVC | Cracks under earth pressure | Only SWR-grade (thick wall) PVC |
| 90° bends underground | Causes blockages, impossible to rod later | Only 45° bends, max 2 total |
| Rubber ring joints underground | Can shift, leak into earth over time | Solvent cement joints only |
| Pipe sitting on stones/rocks | Point loading cracks the pipe | Sand bed (6") under entire length |
| Floor trap too high/low | Either stands proud of floor or creates a puddle | Set with spirit level, matching floor height |
| Soak pit too close to building | Moisture seeps into foundation, weakens plinth | Minimum 10 feet away |
| Soak pit near borewell | Contamination of drinking water | Minimum 50 feet from any water source |
| No vent pipe provision | Toilet will flush slowly, gurgling sound | Leave space in the wall for 2" vent pipe (goes up later) |
| Forgetting rain drain exits | Cannot add downpipes later without breaking plinth | Embed 4" PVC in plinth wall NOW |

## SUMMARY: TOP 5 THINGS THAT NEED MOST ATTENTION

### 🔴 1. DRAIN PIPE SLOPE (1:40)
This is the #1 failure point. If slope is wrong:
- Toilet won't flush
- Sewage sits in pipe, smells
- Cannot fix without breaking floor
**How to verify:** Pour water at toilet stub. It must flow smoothly to soak pit in under 10 seconds for the full length. No pooling.

### 🔴 2. SOAK PIT DISTANCE + CONSTRUCTION
- Must be 10ft+ from building (moisture weakens foundation)
- Must be 50ft+ from borewell (contamination risk)
- Must have HONEYCOMB brickwork (NOT solid — water needs to seep out)
- Must have NO concrete bottom (needs to drain into earth)

### 🔴 3. PIPE BEDDING (SAND)
The pipe sits underground with earth fill above it. Without a sand bed:
- Stones/rocks create point loads
- Pipe cracks over time
- Leak goes unnoticed until smell/blockage
**Insist on:** 6" sand below + 6" sand above pipe before earth fill.

### 🔴 4. FLOOR TRAP POSITION + LEVEL
Mark the EXACT position on the PCC surface:
- If too high → stands above floor = trip hazard
- If too low → creates a depression = water pools
- If wrong position → commode won't align later
**Get the plumber to mark it with the mistri TOGETHER** — both need to agree on position.

### 🔴 5. TESTING BEFORE POUR
**Non-negotiable:** 24 hours before floor slab pour:
- Water test entire drain line
- Check every joint
- Verify flow to soak pit
- Take photos of everything
Once concrete goes over it — it's sealed forever. No second chance.
